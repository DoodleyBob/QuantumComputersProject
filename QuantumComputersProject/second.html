The Principles:

There are four main Principles in Quantum Computing:

Superposition:   The State in which a quantum particle or system can represent multuple different possibilities.
Entanglement:   The process in which multiple quantum particles become correlated more strongly than probablility allows.
Decoherence:   The process in which quantum particles and systems can decay, collapse or change, changing into single states that can be measured by classic computers.
Decoherence:   Interference is the phenomenon in which entangled quantum states can interact and produce more and less likely probabilities.
